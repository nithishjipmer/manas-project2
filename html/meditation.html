Pranayama

Anulom Vilom Pranayama, also known as Alternate Nostril Breathing, is a yogic breathing technique that helps balance the flow of energy in the body and calm the mind.

  • Sit down in a comfortable and quiet place. You can sit on the floor or on a chair with your back straight.
  • Place your left hand on your left knee with the palm facing upward.
  • Use your right thumb to close your right nostril.
  • Close your eyes gently.
  • Inhale deeply and slowly through your left nostril while keeping your right nostril closed with your thumb.
  • Fill your lungs completely with air.
  • After inhaling, close your left nostril with your ring finger, keeping your right nostril closed with your thumb.
  • Hold your breath for a brief moment.
  • Release your right nostril (thumb) and exhale slowly and completely through your right nostril.
  • Ensure that you exhale all the air from your lungs.
  • Inhale deeply and slowly through your right nostril.
  • Fill your lungs with air while keeping your left nostril closed with your ring finger.
  • After inhaling, close your right nostril (thumb) and hold your breath briefly.
  • Release your left nostril (ring finger) and exhale slowly and completely through your left nostril.
  • Release your left nostril (ring finger) and exhale slowly and completely through your left nostril.
  • This completes one cycle of Anulom Vilom Pranayama
  • Repeat this cycle for a desired number of rounds, usually starting with 5-10 rounds and gradually increasing as you become more comfortable.
  • After completing the desired number of rounds, release your hand mudra.
  • Sit quietly for a few moments with your eyes closed, focusing on your breath and its calming effects.
